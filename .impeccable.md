# Mainstay — Impeccable Design Guidelines

This file is the source of truth for design intent on Mainstay. The `impeccable:*` skills read it to keep design decisions consistent across every session and surface.

## Design Context

### Users
Mainstay is two-sided:
- **Owners** — small, local store operators using Mainstay's admin tools to run their shop (orders, inventory, store presence). They are often non-technical, time-pressed, and value clarity over power-user density.
- **Shoppers** — neighbors and locals browsing and buying from independent stores. They expect a calm, trustworthy shopping experience that feels like the store itself, not a marketplace.

The job-to-be-done: help a local business feel as confident and credible online as it does on its own street, and help shoppers feel they're supporting a real neighbor.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **warm, trustworthy, neighborly.**

Voice and tone: human and direct, never corporate. Talks like a thoughtful shopkeeper, not a platform. Uses plain language; avoids growth-hacky superlatives and SaaS jargon. Confidence comes from craft and clarity, not from shouting.

Emotional goal: shoppers feel welcomed and at ease; owners feel respected and capable.

### Aesthetic Direction
Borrow from two reference families and blend them:
- **Shopify / Square** for commerce pragmatism — familiar shopping patterns, clear product/price hierarchy, fast paths to action.
- **Notion / Figma** for personality — confident type, gentle illustration, real character in empty states and microcopy.

**Visual palette:** light theme only. Cream / off-white backgrounds, warm neutral surfaces (paper, sand, stone), deep readable text. One confident warm accent (terracotta, forest green, or mustard) used for primary CTAs and highlights — not decoration. Plenty of breathing room.

**Typography:** humanist sans for UI, with optional editorial serif accents for store names, section titles, or marketing surfaces — never both in the same density. Generous line-height; comfortable reading at default sizes.

**Anti-references (explicit don'ts):**
- No corporate/enterprise SaaS chrome (cold blues, dense data grids, generic dashboards).
- No crypto/tech-bro neon, no dark futurism.
- No big-box retail density (Amazon-style stuffed pages, banner clutter).
- No generic AI-app gradient soup (purple→pink gradients, glassmorphism, "GPT wrapper" aesthetic).

### Accessibility
Target **WCAG AA** across the product:
- Text contrast ≥ 4.5:1 (≥ 3:1 for large text and UI components).
- Visible, generous focus indicators on every interactive element.
- Full keyboard navigation; no mouse-only flows.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — provide static alternatives for any decorative motion.
- Color is never the only carrier of meaning; pair with icon, label, or shape.

### Design Principles
1. **Local before global.** Every screen should feel like *this* store, not a template. Owner branding, store voice, and product photography lead; Mainstay chrome recedes.
2. **Warmth is a feature, not decoration.** Use cream surfaces, soft contrast, human copy, and real photography. Avoid synthetic gradients, stock illustrations, and decorative chrome.
3. **One confident accent.** A single warm color carries action and emphasis. If everything is highlighted, nothing is. Color earns its place.
4. **Calm density.** Storefronts and dashboards both prioritize breathable layouts and clear hierarchy over information cramming. When in doubt, remove.
5. **Accessible by default.** WCAG AA is a floor, not a goal. Contrast, focus, motion, and keyboard parity are non-negotiable.
